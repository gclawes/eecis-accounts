Register an EE/CIS Account

Information and Guidelines for your Computer Account

  1. All persons receiving accounts must submit a valid account form electronically, with faculty approval. All questions must be answered in order to process your request.

  2. The account will normally be activated within 24 hours from the time of receiving a valid, faculty approved form.

  3. Your login will be your last name, or the first eight characters of your last name, whichever is shorter. Note that someone may already have an account with the same name as you. When this happens, the account is given the first character(s) of your first name, and your last name, with the total length limited to eight characters.

  4. Your initial password will be chosen by you. The form will enforce some rules so you will choose a strong password.

  5. All University computing resources are under the auspice of the Policy for Responsible Computing. If you use the lab facilities in an inappropriate manner, your computing privileges will be revoked. In addition the offender(s) may also be taken before the campus Judicial Board. Examples of using lab facilities in an inappropriate manner include, but are not restricted to the following:

    • Allowing someone else to use your account.

    • Accessing files without permission. Note that you should get permission from the owner of a file before you access a file. Just because you are able to read a file on the system does not mean that you have been given permission to read the file.

    • Writing, copying, or storing programs that can be used to violate the privacy of other users, even if these programs are never used.

  6. The Lab Administration reserves the right to affect any part of the system deemed necessary, by them, to maintain the system integrity.

  7. The Lab Administration reserves the right to monitor any part of the network deemed necessary, by them, to maintain the network integrity. Furthermore, by agreeing to the account terms, you authorized the network data collected ( which may include your traffic) to be used for Research Purposes.

  8. For a research account your account will expire after you have left the University. For a class account your account will expire at the start of the following semester and it is your responsibility to renew your account through sequential semesters, or your account and files will be considered abandoned. The files that remain on the system will become the property of the University of Delaware, and the University may use or dispose of the files in whatever manner they see fit. Any files that you want to keep private should be deleted before your account expires. Tape drives are available to make copies of your files before you leave the University.

  9. You should have received a New User Guide with this form that explains more things about the ECE/CIS labs.
By applying for an account, I agree to abide by the University Computing Security Policies, and follow University Responsible Computing Guidelines and those contained herein. Additionally, I agree to abide and enforce the mandates for Protecting Personal Non-Public Information (PNPI) for myself, and any Faculty/Student/Staff data that I may come in contact with.
